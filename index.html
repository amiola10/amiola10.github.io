Growth Mindset

Reflections & Lessons Learned

Cybersecurity is a field that demands continuous learning. Here is how my understanding has evolved.

01
Skills Gained
This course has sharpened my ability to think like both a defender and an attacker. I have developed practical fluency in network forensics, log analysis, OSINT methodology, and security hardening — skills that complement my existing IT support background and move me closer to a SOC analyst role.
02
Challenges Encountered
The most significant challenge was bridging theory and real-world application. Early CTF competitions exposed gaps in my scripting ability and forensics tooling knowledge. I addressed these by dedicating extra time to Cyber Skyline practice modules and independent research, gradually improving my performance across competition rounds.
03
Evolving Understanding
I entered this program thinking cybersecurity was primarily about tools and technology. I now understand it is equally about process, communication, and human behavior. Threat actors exploit people as much as systems. This human-centered view has made me a more holistic security professional.
04
Areas for Continued Growth
I am actively working to deepen my proficiency in scripting for security automation (Python), cloud security concepts (AWS/Azure), and SIEM platform experience. My goal is to earn a SOC-specific certification and gain hands-on SOC internship experience before graduating from the University of Houston.
